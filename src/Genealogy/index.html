Keys Family Genealogy

This Keys Family genealogy has been gathered by Phillip M. Keys, philm@keysfamily.org.

Keys Family Genealogy notes is a short description of the family. This note was received from my grandfather.

His great-great-grandfather was Oliver B. Hayden who was disowned by father after disserting from British Army during War of 1812. After being taken in by a Scottish Presbyterian minister named Keys, he changed name to George Keys.

His son was David Steele Keys, a civil war veteran who died early of his injuries.

His son was Edward McClellan Keys, who was a sourdough in the Yukon and Alaska together with his wife Georgianna Lesher and his three sons Edward M., George L. and Ralph H.

Page 57 of the Annual Report of the Superintendent of Soldier's Orphans, 1877 lists Edward M. Keys as graduating at the age of sixteen from the Mount Joy School in Pennsylvania.

My grandfather was Edward McClellan Keys II.

My great-uncle, George L. Keys, wrote Going to the Klondike and Memoirs of George L. Keys. Alaska days recalled is an article about him and his wife LarVern. Secretary recalls frontier college describes LarVern Keys, nee Borell recollections about the founding of the Alaska Agricultural College and School of Mines now the University of Alaska.

My father was Phillip McClellan Keys.

I, his son, am Phillip McClelland Keys.
